- Retro Gaming -

Modern video games are bigger than ever before, with shiny graphics, realistic physics, high-quality sound, and an amazing overall level of detail all brought about through the efforts of development teams much larger than those of the past. In turn, these games are powered by computing hardware that is in some respects 1,000 times more capable than that which existed twenty years ago. Graphics hardware in particular has come along in leaps and bounds, with PC graphics cards now on the market that are bigger than an average games console, all for the purpose of handling the ever-increasing demands of detailed geometry and complex shading demanded of them by the latest games.

There's no denying that these games are impressive and that many of them provide a remarkable experience for the gamers that enjoy them, but when it comes to a game being fun or memorable, more significant factors may be at work than "realism" and "eye candy".

Of course one might just assume that retro-gaming is purely about nostalgia and that's definitely a factor, but the appeal of playing 'retro' titles must go beyond that. Some games are great to play simply because of the rewarding gameplay itself, or because of the engaging story. Sometimes the simplest games are the most enjoyable.

For older games, the most common way of accessing these titles is through emulation, although more committed retro-gamers may seek out original hardware.

Not all 'retro' games are old though. There are many modern titles, especially from "indie" developers, that intentionally make use of a 'retro' aesthetic, for example by using low-resolution 'pixel art', a limited colour palette, or simple sound effects that evoke the experience of a much older gaming title. These games vary from having a purely 'retro' feel to having a mix of both modern and 'retro' elements, often taking advantage of the greater graphical capabilties on modern systems. Many of them also fall into the "Metroidvania" genre (so called because they take elements from the classic 80s games 'Metroid' and 'Castlevania').

Finally, the culture of retro-gaming extends beyond websites and online forums to exhibitions, museums, and even a printed magazine!
